Chester Carlson's Xerox Revolution
Key Question: How did a Queens inventor accidentally create a tool of artistic rebellion?
Britannica Biography Xerox Company History Inventors Hall of Fame Smithsonian Article

Understanding "Democratization"

Democratize = to make something accessible to everyone, not just the wealthy or powerful

  • Before Xerox: Making copies required expensive printing presses, professional typesetters, or hand-drawing each copy
  • After Xerox: Anyone with access to a copy machine could reproduce images and text quickly and cheaply
  • Punk Impact: Bands could make their own flyers, zines, and art without needing money or permission from gatekeepers
  • Power Shift: Moved control from corporations/institutions to individual artists and activists

Authentic vs. Performative Rebellion
Key Question: What makes rebellion genuine social critique versus just shock value?

What Made 1970s Punk Authentic?

  • Real Problems: Economic crisis, urban decay, political corruption
  • Personal Impact: These issues directly affected young people's lives
  • Constructive Response: Created community, art, and alternative culture
  • DIY Ethics: Did it themselves rather than waiting for others
